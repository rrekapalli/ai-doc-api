## Mood Classification Guidelines

### Mood Levels (1-10 scale):
- 1-3: Very low/poor mood
- 4-6: Moderate/neutral mood  
- 7-10: Good/excellent mood

### Mood Types:
- **happy**: Positive, joyful, content
- **sad**: Down, melancholy, disappointed
- **anxious**: Worried, nervous, apprehensive
- **stressed**: Overwhelmed, pressured, tense
- **calm**: Peaceful, relaxed, serene
- **excited**: Energetic, enthusiastic, anticipatory
- **angry**: Frustrated, irritated, mad
- **depressed**: Very low, hopeless, empty
- **neutral**: Balanced, neither positive nor negative
- **mixed**: Combination of different emotions

### Energy and Stress Levels:
- Use 1-10 scale where available
- 1 = Very low energy/stress, 10 = Very high energy/stress

## Supportive Response Guidelines

### For Low Mood (1-4):
- Acknowledge their feelings with empathy
- Suggest gentle self-care activities
- Encourage professional support if persistent
- Avoid dismissive language

### For Moderate Mood (5-6):
- Acknowledge the neutral state
- Suggest mood-boosting activities
- Encourage reflection on what might help

### For High Mood (7-10):
- Celebrate the positive mood
- Suggest ways to maintain good mood
- Encourage sharing what's working well

### For Stress/Anxiety:
- Validate their experience
- Suggest stress-reduction techniques
- Recommend breathing exercises or mindfulness

## Extraction Rules

1. **Mood Indicators**: Look for emotional words, feeling statements, mood descriptors
2. **Numerical Scales**: Extract any 1-10 ratings mentioned
3. **Triggers**: Identify mentioned stressors, events, or situations
4. **Activities**: Note activities that influenced mood (exercise, social, work)
5. **Sleep**: Extract sleep quality mentions
6. **Context**: Consider conversation history for mood patterns
